# The Pomodoro Technique for Deep Focus

## What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It uses a timer to break work into focused intervals (traditionally 25 minutes), separated by short breaks.

## How It Works

1. **Choose a task** you want to work on
2. **Set a timer** for 25 minutes (one "Pomodoro")
3. **Work focused** until the timer rings
4. **Take a 5-minute break** - stand up, stretch, hydrate
5. **After 4 Pomodoros**, take a longer 15-30 minute break

## Why It Works for Focus

- **Time pressure creates urgency**: Knowing you only have 25 minutes motivates you to stay on task
- **Breaks prevent burnout**: Regular rest periods maintain mental energy throughout the day
- **Measurable progress**: Counting completed Pomodoros provides a sense of accomplishment
- **Eliminates decision fatigue**: No need to decide "should I take a break?" - the system decides for you

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Skipping Breaks
Many beginners try to power through multiple Pomodoros without breaks. This leads to:
- Decreased focus quality
- Mental fatigue
- Reduced productivity in later sessions

**Solution**: Honor the break time. Even if you're "in the zone," stepping away prevents burnout.

### 2. Checking Phone During Work Time
The point of a Pomodoro is *uninterrupted* focus. Every phone check resets your concentration.

**Solution**: Put your phone in another room or use FocusGuard's distraction monitoring to hold yourself accountable.

### 3. Choosing Tasks That Are Too Large
If a task takes 10 Pomodoros, you won't feel progress for hours.

**Solution**: Break large tasks into 25-minute subtasks. Instead of "Write report," try "Outline introduction section."

## Optimizing for Your Focus Type

### Morning People
- Schedule hardest tasks in your first 2-3 Pomodoros
- Use early focus for creative/strategic work
- Reserve afternoon Pomodoros for routine tasks

### Night Owls
- Warm up with easier tasks in morning
- Save deep work for evening Pomodoros when focus peaks
- Adjust break length based on energy levels
