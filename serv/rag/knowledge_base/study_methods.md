# Study Methods

## Active Recall
Active recall is the practice of retrieving information from memory without looking at notes.
It is more effective than rereading or highlighting.

Examples:
- Answering questions from memory
- Writing everything you know about a topic on a blank page
- Using flashcards

Best use:
- Exam preparation
- Memorization-heavy subjects

---

## Spaced Repetition
Spaced repetition means reviewing material at increasing intervals over time.
This improves long-term retention.

Typical schedule:
- Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 21 → Day 60

Tools:
- Anki
- Custom flashcard systems

---

## Feynman Technique
A learning technique where you explain a concept in simple language as if teaching a beginner.

Steps:
1. Choose a concept
2. Explain it simply
3. Identify gaps
4. Refine explanation

Best for:
- Conceptual understanding
- Detecting weak points
